Free Queue Growth & Fairness

Four grounded directions for turning Daily Free from a nightly re-enrollment chore into a persistent, trustworthy path to play—while favoring people who have waited longest without locking them into repetitive cohorts.

What is true today

Grounding Context

Codebase Context

/games/free already contains the focused queue journey: public count and countdown, sign-in, create-agent empty state, agent picker, join/leave, today’s game, and season standings. Homepage navigation and status link there; dashboard mission control already selects create, join, view queue, or view game from loaded state. There is no global growth modal, though accessible modal patterns exist.

Enrollment contract

free_game_queue stores only ID, unique account, agent, and join time. REST validates ownership, permits one agent per account, and requires leaving before switching; MCP offers idempotent same-agent join and leave-when-absent. Queue and active-game enrollment are mutually exclusive. Reads expose count, the owner’s entry, estimated draw, latest game, and random-draw—not odds, priority, or miss history.

Draw mechanics

A transaction lock enforces one draw per UTC day. With at least two queued humans, the scheduler uses entries.sort(() => Math.random() - 0.5), takes up to 12, creates owned seats, and fills remaining seats with House agents. Picked rows are deleted; unpicked rows retain their original join time. Played agents are not re-added automatically, and no seed, priority score, or draw receipt exists.

Product and policy boundaries

Daily Free is part of the persistent agent loop and can feed Season 0 standings. Queue selection priority must remain separate from public championship points and producer-only competition rating. Management surfaces may prepare owned agents and enroll them before play, never control active matches. Persistent policy also needs explicit behavior for manual leave, agent deletion or editing, season boundaries, and failed or cancelled games.

External evidence

Automatic defaults increase participation when paired with visible opt-out and editable preferences. Game matchmaking and service schedulers use request age and priority aging to prevent starvation. Work-conserving fair queues deprioritize recently served participants only during contention, using them when capacity would otherwise sit idle. Tournament waitlists show that capacity, governing timestamp, standing, cutoff, and final explanation are part of the fairness contract.

Core tension

Growth, persistence, newcomer preference, and longest-wait priority can conflict. Queue membership and tonight’s selection are currently collapsed but need not be. Literal ticket multipliers become opaque and unbounded; literal zero-chance classes can strand humans behind House seats. The operator needs adjustable Season 0 policy, while players need a promise stable enough to understand.

Supporting scope: practical admin visibility

Do not turn Daily Free into league infrastructure. A small admin queue table is enough: eligible count, seats available, longest current wait, and one row per agent showing status, last game, consecutive eligible misses, and broad priority. Selected players are already visible in the game. Unit and property tests should validate selector invariants; bespoke policy versions, draw ledgers, counterfactuals, and player-facing algorithm detail wait until real scale or stakes create a concrete need.

Persistent enrollment and consent

1. Standing Daily Agent + Immutable Nightly Candidates

Description: Replace nightly queue membership with a standing owner preference: one designated Daily Agent remains eligible until paused or replaced. At each cutoff, create an immutable nightly candidate snapshot containing agent, eligibility, participation provenance, and policy inputs. Selection consumes the snapshot—not the standing preference—so playing no longer deletes consent and “automatic re-entry” disappears as a special action.

Conceptual separation: durable participation creates tonight’s candidates; the draw never consumes the owner’s underlying choice.

Axis

Basis
direct: today one four-field row collapses participation and candidacy, and picked rows are deleted at draw (packages/api/src/db/schema.ts:1540-1551; packages/api/src/routes/free-queue.ts:380-386). Owner-scoped, idempotent queue mutation already exists. external: automatic-enrollment evidence pairs persistence with real opt-out and editable defaults.
Rationale
This removes the recurring action that suppresses participation while creating the correct boundary for wait state and consent. It directly supports the persistent-agent product loop.
Downsides
Requires a new participation lifecycle and migration from current rows. Pause, agent replacement, deletion, active-game conflicts, season transitions, and cancelled games need explicit semantics. Persistent eligibility can inflate inactive demand unless paired with idea 4.

2
Scarce-slot selection fairness

2. Aged Lottery With Mix Protection

Description: Treat waiting as a bounded priority signal, not a deterministic roster order. Reserve only part of the 12 seats, with explicit bounded quotas for first-game candidates and highest-debt waiters; unused quota returns to the general pool. Fill every remaining human seat through a seeded weighted lottery without replacement, where each eligible miss raises—but does not dictate—selection odds. While assembling the lottery portion, apply a recent-opponent multiplier with a positive floor so repeated pairings become less likely but no eligible candidate is excluded. If 12 or fewer humans are eligible, seat them all before adding House agents.

Conceptual balance: protect a bounded share of seats for access fairness, then use reproducible randomness and recent-opponent history to resist cohort phase-locking.

Axis
Scarce-slot selection fairness
Basis
direct: only joinedAt exists today and selection is an unreproducible random-comparator shuffle (packages/api/src/db/schema.ts:1549-1551; packages/api/src/routes/free-queue.ts:297-305). external: established schedulers use aging, while work-conserving fair queues preserve access without hard exclusion. reasoned: with exactly 36 stable players, strict highest-debt-first selection can phase-lock into the same three cohorts of 12; partial seat protection plus weighted mixing decouples service order from full-roster composition.
Rationale
Waiting still produces meaningful progress, but deterministic priority no longer causes the same players to advance as an intact block every third draw. The selector optimizes two distinct outcomes: timely access and varied opponents.
Downsides
The two protected quotas, debt cap, lottery weights, pairing-history window, diversity floor, pause behavior, and newcomer definition all require simulation. Too much randomness weakens perceived progress; too much mix protection can delay service. Randomized mixing reduces but cannot guarantee against repeated cohorts, and the system still cannot promise a finite wait under permanently excessive demand.

Acquisition prompts and agent creation

3. Queue Growth State Machine

Description: Replace a generic global pop-up with one state-aware next action on high-intent surfaces. Agentless owners create inline; owners with agents activate a suggested Daily Agent in one action; active members see status or pause; selected owners go to the game; completed owners see the next eligibility state. Trigger only after sign-in, dashboard return, agent creation, or a completed-game moment—not during live play or replay.

Axis
Acquisition prompts and agent creation
Basis
direct: dashboard mission control and /games/free already branch among agentless, unenrolled, queued, and active states (packages/web/src/app/dashboard/dashboard-mission-control.ts:135-177; packages/web/src/app/games/free/free-game-content.tsx:129-203). Modal and provider seams exist, but no queue-growth overlay does.
Rationale
A routing pop-up adds interruption while leaving the work intact. Completing the smallest valid transition in place converts existing state awareness into acquisition and reduces steps to a real enrollment.
Downsides
Cross-surface coordination, dismissal/frequency caps, auth readiness, and race-safe mutations add complexity. The suggested agent needs a predictable rule, and inline creation can become a second form implementation if scoped poorly.

Persistent enrollment and consent

4. Renewable Membership With a Dormancy Brake

Description: Keep standing participation active through games, but model liveness explicitly. Membership moves among active, paused, and needs-renewal states; manual leave is a durable pause, while bounded inactivity evidence or a season boundary can request one-click renewal. Agent deletion/unavailability pauses immediately instead of leaving an orphaned candidacy.

Axis
Persistent enrollment and consent
Basis
direct: the four-field queue row cannot represent consent lifecycle or liveness, agent state already includes queue/active enrollment, and queue foreign keys have no delete action (packages/api/src/services/agent-profile-management.ts:620-648; packages/api/src/db/schema.ts:1540-1551). external: persistent defaults retain choice through visible opt-out and later edits.
Rationale
This captures the growth benefit of inertia without letting visible demand become a cemetery of abandoned agents. It makes the meaning and duration of consent inspectable.
Downsides
Choosing liveness signals is sensitive: opening results or returning to the app may be too strict, while time-only expiry may be arbitrary. Renewal prompts can recreate the original churn if too frequent. Notification data may not exist yet.
