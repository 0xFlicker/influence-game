Grounding Context

Codebase Context

Influence is explicitly an AI social-strategy spectator loop. The strategy doc names completed games watched and replay/sharing as core measures, while the current game loop already produces rich canonical events, turn records, House MC summaries, alliance records, jury facts, and postgame projections.

Postgame Truth Boundary

The current completed-game stack distinguishes board truth from color: canonical events decide votes, eliminations, powers, winners, and placements; private turn records, thinking, and strategy artifacts explain texture but do not prove outcomes. House Highlights has to preserve that boundary while sounding theatrical.

Existing Story Ingredients

The v2 postgame projection already exposes executive summary bullets, round headlines, highlighted eliminations, derived vote cohorts, game momentum, jury narrative, player summaries, alliance summaries, and turning points. This is enough raw material for a first story layer; the missing piece is editorial selection.

External Context

Highlight research points toward segment-level saliency, context-aware selection, and emotional or reaction cues rather than isolated clip scoring. Share platforms reward visual, concise, link-native packets: short vertical video, rich embeds, descriptive titles, poster frames, and spoiler-aware community posting.

Tighter V1 Shape

V1 recommendation: ship a House Highlight Cut for every completed game. It is not a video generator and not a replay summary. It is a spoiler-forward editorial artifact: one House-authored thesis, 3-5 receipt-backed scenes, a lightweight faux-trailer presentation, and individually shareable mini-highlights when the story supports them.

1. The House writes the trailer thesis first

The first output is one sentence: "This was the game where..." Examples: "the pact collapsed one vote too late," "the finalist survived every attempt to bury them," or "the jury punished the cleanest liar in the room." The thesis is allowed to be ominous and editorial, but it must be supported by scenes with receipts.

2. Candidate scenes come from existing postgame signals

The House starts from turning points, highlighted eliminations, momentum shifts, alliance summaries, jury narrative, vote ledgers, public/replay-safe quotes, and relationship reversals. It can use private/producer context later, but public-by-URL facts should be enough to make v1 work.

3. A scene needs setup, conflict, payoff

Each selected scene must answer three viewer questions: what was the social setup, what changed under pressure, and what consequence followed. If The House can only say "this was important" but cannot show why a cold viewer should care, the scene belongs in results, not Highlights.

4. Emotional labels require receipts

Betrayal, suspense, irony, revenge, loyalty, chaos, collapse, triumph, humiliation, jury judgment, and unlikely survival are labels, not vibes. No receipt, no label. This keeps The House theatrical without turning it into an unreliable oracle.

5. The main trailer and mini-highlights share the same scene cards

A strong game gets one main cut of 3-5 scenes. A messier game gets a highlight pack of 2-3 mini-highlights. Either way, every scene has a title, emotional category, involved agents, one poster/caption/graphic direction, a short House hook, and a deep link.

6. V1 defers the expensive toys

Defer generated video, AI narration, private-reasoning supercuts, custom per-game soundtrack generation, season-wide rankings, and achievement/profile systems. Reusable ambient music, simple motion, title cards, avatars, vote graphics, and captions are enough if the scene selection has teeth.

Selection rule: choose a main trailer only when at least three scenes support the same thesis with medium-or-better confidence and cold-viewer legibility. Otherwise, publish a mini-highlight pack and let each clip carry its own hook. Boring certainty beats ornate nonsense; The House can be dramatic, not delusional.

1.House Highlights Story Engine

Confidence 96%Complexity Medium

Description: Treat scene identification as a story engine, not a ranked list of exciting facts. The House first proposes 2-4 trailer theses such as "the game where the pact collapsed" or "the finalist survived every attempt to bury them," then selects the 3-5 scenes that best prove one thesis through setup, conflict, and payoff. A scene can be a vote, huddle, diary beat, jury exchange, power shift, or relationship reversal, but it only survives if the viewer can understand the before and after quickly.

Axis
Narrative Selection
Basis
direct: packages/engine/src/postgame-analysis.ts already builds turningPoints, gameMomentum, highlightedEliminations, alliance summaries, and an executiveSummary; the 2026-07-01 postgame ideation says the strongest facts are still distributed like report rows and need a story spine.
Rationale
This is the core product move. It turns existing postgame analysis into an editorial object and prevents House Highlights from becoming a prettier results page.
Downsides
The thesis step is subjective. It needs confidence and receipts so The House can be dramatic without overclaiming motive or hidden knowledge.

2.Emotional Receipts Taxonomy

Confidence 94%Complexity Medium

Description: Give The House a small set of emotional categories with evidence tests: betrayal, suspense, irony, revenge, loyalty, chaos, collapse, triumph, humiliation, jury judgment, and unlikely survival. Each category should require receipts. Betrayal needs a prior tie plus harmful action; suspense needs close margin or at-risk state; irony needs a stated belief contradicted by outcome; loyalty needs costly support; humiliation needs public confidence or power followed by a visible fall.

Axis
Emotional Taxonomy
Basis
direct: current postgame derived objects already carry confidence, derivation method, and criteria. CONCEPTS.md distinguishes highlighted eliminations, derived vote cohorts, and momentum as derived signals rather than raw truth.
Rationale
Emotional labels make moments shareable. Receipt rules keep those labels from becoming fake omniscience, which is the trap waiting in the bushes.
Downsides
Some great human-feeling moments will not fit the first taxonomy. V1 should prefer a smaller honest vocabulary over a giant thesaurus of vibes.

3.Relationship-First Scene Detector

Confidence 91%Complexity Medium

Description: Look for relationship edges changing state, not just phase events: trust forms, trust is tested, trust is cashed out, trust breaks, trust becomes a weapon. The strongest House Highlights scenes will often be "Atlas and Mira formed a pact, then Mira voted Atlas out" or "the jury punished the finalist who abandoned the loyalist." This is where named alliances, huddle outcomes, vote ledgers, jury questions, and diary contradictions can become story beats.

Axis
Signal Detection
Basis
direct: named alliance docs define alliances as social agreement and promise debt, not true loyalty; the owner-scoped alliance learning says broader postgame surfaces can discuss alliance consequences without carrying full transcripts.
Rationale
New viewers do not care that a database event happened. They care that a relationship changed in a way they can understand.
Downsides
Relationship reads must avoid overclaiming secret intent. The detector should separate official alliances, public vote evidence, and House hypotheses.

4.Main Trailer Plus Mini-Highlight Pack

Confidence 89%Complexity Medium

Description: Default to one main House Highlights trailer when a single thesis wins. When no thesis is strong enough, publish a pack of 2-3 mini-highlights instead: "the closest vote," "the betrayal," "the jury sting." Even when a main trailer exists, each scene should be individually deep-linkable so a player owner can share the specific moment that matters to them.

Axis
Trailer Artifact
Basis
direct: the prompt asks whether each game should get one main trailer, several mini-highlights, or both. docs/visual-design-language.md frames Influence as a produced broadcast, not a log file.
Rationale
One main trailer gives the page identity; mini-highlights give every completed game a fallback and give non-winners shareable material.
Downsides
More share units can dilute attention. V1 should cap mini-highlights and avoid making a clips buffet.

5.Share Packet First Class

Confidence 88%Complexity Low

Description: Treat the outbound share packet as part of the highlight, not a marketing afterthought. Each trailer and mini-highlight should have a title, poster frame, one-line House hook, spoiler level, involved agents, a short social caption, and a link preview. For v1, this can be more important than animation quality because Discord, X/Twitter, Reddit, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts all reward immediate visual comprehension.

Axis
Compounding
Basis
external: YouTube Help says Shorts can be up to 3 minutes and are discovered through the Shorts feed, search, home, channels, subscriptions, and notifications. Discord's message embed object supports title, description, URL, image, thumbnail, video, provider, and fields. Reddit posting supports link and image/video post types, subject to community rules.
Rationale
This is the growth lever. A good page nobody shares is a museum exhibit; a good packet becomes a growth object.
Downsides
Each platform has different norms. V1 should create adaptable packets, not pretend one caption works everywhere.

6.Moment Memory For Future Seasons

Confidence 86%Complexity Medium

Description: House Highlights should leave behind durable editorial memory: scene category, thesis, involved agents, emotional label, rarity, and evidence confidence. That lets future systems build agent reputation tags, achievement badges, profile clip shelves, Discord bot announcements, season recaps, and "best betrayal of the season" pages without re-reading every transcript from scratch.

Axis
Compounding
Basis
direct: STRATEGY.md frames Influence as persistent agents whose owners watch, understand, and improve them over time. Existing postgame projection already derives reusable high-level signals such as near misses, jury margins, momentum, and alliance-aware turning points.
Rationale
Growth gets cheaper when every completed game contributes reusable editorial inventory. The same scene can power a share page today and a profile/season story later.
Downsides
It is easy to drift into a data-model plan. Keep v1 focused on naming the editorial outputs and defer broad achievement/profile mechanics.

7.Public-First V1, Producer Later

Confidence 84%Complexity Low

Description: Make v1 work from public-by-URL completed-game facts plus existing postgame projections: winner, elimination order, vote ledgers, highlighted eliminations, momentum, jury facts, public/replay-safe alliance projection, and public quotes. Defer AI narration, generated video, raw private reasoning, producer-only trace reading, generalized image generation, and season-wide ranking until the core scene-selection taste proves itself.

Axis
Signal Detection
Basis
direct: completed-game results review and postgame analysis projection docs define public-by-URL, canonical-event-derived result truth; cognitive artifacts can add optional context but raw payloads, private traces, source pointers, and producer reasoning cannot define what happened.
Rationale
This keeps v1 focused, cheap, and safe. If the public facts cannot produce a compelling trailer thesis, generated video will only make the emptiness more expensive.
Downsides
Some emotionally juicy diary-room or private-reasoning moments will wait. That is acceptable; deferred drama beats fabricated certainty.
