Post-Vote Mingle Drama

A focused ideation pass on removing RUMOR from the normal live loop, moving MINGLE after VOTE, and giving players vote-result pressure without commanding them to perform drama.

Date

Mode

Primary Goal
Emergent social pressure
Recommended Slice
Vote-locked Mingle

Product thesis

The vote should close before the social consequences close.

The strongest version of the tweak is not simply "move Mingle later." It is a new suspense beat: players vote first, then everyone sees enough fallout to know who has leverage, who is in trouble, and who can still be saved or redirected before Power resolves.

That creates a cleaner reason for the desired behavior. At-risk players can plead because the danger is real. Players can suck up because the empowered player is known. Players can name targets because replacement risk is visible. The prompt does not need to say "plead" or "flatter"; the game state makes those moves useful.

Recommended rule shape Normal live loop: LOBBY -> VOTE -> MINGLE -> POWER -> COUNCIL. Keep setup, introduction, and replay compatibility as separate concerns.

Phase move

From pre-vote speculation to post-vote bargaining

Grounding

What the repo already says

  • The phase machine currently completes LOBBY -> MINGLE -> RUMOR -> VOTE -> POWER.
  • RUMOR is isolated as a phase, but tests, viewer transitions, labels, and docs mirror it.
  • Expose scores, empowered-player exclusion, shield removal, and candidate backfill already exist in game state.
  • Mingle prompts already allow a strategy spectrum and explicitly do not require naming a target.
  • Existing Power Lobby already has the closest version of "locked vote, provisional danger."
  • Turn artifacts and MCP/read-model surfaces can validate whether behavior actually changed.
Important boundary This ideation pass treats private reasoning and strategy packets as producer/debug artifacts, not as player-visible content. The live change should expose public-ish pressure state, not hidden chain-of-thought or private plans.

1

Locked-Vote Negotiation Window

Move normal play to LOBBY -> VOTE -> MINGLE -> POWER -> COUNCIL. The vote result becomes a real fact before Mingle starts, but the shield/protect outcome remains open. That makes Mingle feel less like pre-vote chatter and more like bargaining under pressure.

Confidence: high Complexity: medium Core product move
Why now
The requested drama has a natural cause only after players know who is empowered and who is at risk.
Design rule
The vote is locked. Mingle can affect Power and social consequences, not rewrite the vote.
Implementation pull
Update phase-machine order, tests, runner assumptions, viewer pacing, docs, and any Power Lobby overlap.
2

Post-Vote Pressure Projection

Materialize a typed post-vote object once and reuse it everywhere the new beat needs the same truth: Mingle context, House rooming, viewer framing, and validation.

Confidence: high Complexity: medium Best shared abstraction
Include
Empowered player, self status, raw expose pressure, effective at-risk list, shield target possibilities, replacement-risk list, and optional vote visibility facts.
Key distinction
Raw pressure is not the same as effective danger. The empowered player can receive expose votes but should not enter the council candidate pool.
Open dial
How much vote detail to show: scores only, ranks, "voted for you" hints, or full voter-target edges.
3

State, Not Script Mingle Prompting

Rewrite Mingle prompt context around leverage facts rather than desired theater. The prompt can say: who is empowered, your status, who is currently at risk, and who could become at risk if a shield is granted. It should not say: plead, make a deal, suck up, or name a target.

Confidence: high Low product risk Agent quality guardrail
Why it works
Personality, memory, relationships, and self-interest can choose the social move once the board state is legible.
Prompt cleanup
Remove stale "later vote" framing because the vote has already happened when Mingle starts.
Preserve
Keep the existing latitude that players do not have to name a target every turn.
4

Pressure-Aware House Rooms

Give House the same pressure projection when assigning rooms. This lets topology do some of the drama work: at-risk players can end up near the empowered player, replacement-risk players can collide with shield targets, and players with useful leverage can be placed together.

Confidence: high Complexity: medium High leverage
Why it works
Rooming changes who can act on the pressure without making anyone speak a scripted line.
Guardrail
Avoid deterministic recipes. Use pressure as an input, not a hard rule that makes every round look identical.
Nice outcome
The same agents can behave differently because the room gives them different opportunities.
5

Delayed-Consequence Viewer Rail

Removing RUMOR removes a visible public beat. Replace it with a viewer rail that names the new suspense: vote locked, empowered known, at-risk players visible, shield consequence still pending.

Confidence: high Complexity: medium Viewer comprehension
Default text
"The vote is locked. Mingle opens before Power resolves."
Show
Empowered player, current at-risk players, and possible shield displacement in compact public-ish form.
Do not show
Private reasoning, private strategy packets, or hidden raw traces.
6

RUMOR as Legacy Replay, Not Live Phase

Treat RUMOR as removed from normal current games but tolerated for old replays or explicit experiments. This keeps the product decision clear while avoiding unnecessary breakage in historical viewer paths.

Confidence: high Scope control Compatibility only
Keep
Rendering support for existing transcript/event records that contain RUMOR.
Remove
The normal phase transition into RUMOR and docs that describe it as core live play.
Language
Rumors can still emerge as things players say during Mingle; they are no longer a system phase.
7

Emergence Scorecard

Validate by reading structured turn artifacts after sample games. The pass condition is not "every player names a target." It is a healthier mix of pressure-driven behavior than the old loop produced.

Confidence: high Verification No behavior quota
Look for
Protection asks, deal attempts, suspicion shifts, deflection, candid refusal, tactical silence, and targeted appeals to empowered players.
Compare
Old loop sample vs new loop sample using game-N-turns.jsonl, action names, strategy signals, and MCP/read-model queries.
Fail if
Every player sounds like they read the same instruction, or post-vote Mingle does not reference the vote-result pressure at all.

Strategy packets

Where strategy packets fit

Pressure Projection

Shared round state. It says what the table can plausibly know after the vote: empowered player, current risk, raw pressure, and shield displacement.

Strategy Packet

Private player intent. It says how this player is thinking about the pressure: alliance plans, grudges, commitments, reads, and likely moves.

The clean design is to feed pressure projection into the same context surface that already carries memory and strategy artifacts, then let the player decide how to use it. Do not convert the pressure projection itself into a prescriptive "act dramatic" packet.

First implementation slice

Smallest coherent version

Step Change Why it belongs in v1
1 Change normal phase order to LOBBY -> VOTE -> MINGLE -> POWER -> COUNCIL. Without this, Mingle is not responding to real vote pressure.
2 Create the post-vote pressure projection after vote results are known. One typed source prevents prompt, viewer, rooming, and validation from drifting.
3 Feed pressure projection into Mingle prompt context without imperative drama instructions. This is the direct product goal.
4 Give House room assignment access to the same pressure projection. Room topology creates emergent opportunities without scripting lines.
5 Update viewer pacing and docs for the new suspense beat. Players and viewers need the same mental model.
6 Run comparative simulations and inspect structured Mingle behavior. Checks whether drama emerged naturally instead of merely being requested.
Primary product risk If the pressure projection is too complete, dissent may become too punishable and agents may turtle. If it is too vague, Mingle will not have enough traction. Start with current at-risk state plus shield displacement; treat full voter-target visibility as a dial, not a default.
