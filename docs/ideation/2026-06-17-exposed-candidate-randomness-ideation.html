Exposed Candidate Randomness

A focused review of the rule change that replaces opaque exposed-candidate randomness with empowered-player choice where the vote tally leaves ambiguity.

Date: 2026-06-17 Topic: exposed-candidate-randomness Mode: repo-grounded Focus: exposed tie breakers and exposed candidates

Grounding Context

Codebase Context

The current Influence round order is INTRODUCTION -> LOBBY -> VOTE -> MINGLE -> POWER -> REVEAL -> COUNCIL, so the rule change naturally lands after Vote and before/inside Power. Vote already resolves empowerment and builds a reusable post-vote pressure object from alive players, expose scores, and the empowered player.

Current candidate resolution excludes the empowered player and shielded players, then sorts by expose score. The important randomness is the fallback path: when fewer than two candidates remain, the engine picks from eligible players with Math.random(). That is the exact moment this rule change can make social debt visible instead of letting the system silently fill.

The prompt surface is ready for this kind of change. Agent prompts already render named sections for Game Rules, Current Stakes, Revealed Vote Ledger, Post-Vote Pressure, and Room-Specific Social Opportunity. The strongest implementation shape is therefore not a new phase or an experiment framework; it is a typed rule object shared by candidate selection, pressure projection, prompts, canonical events, and simulation review.

External context was light but directionally useful: recent social-deduction voting research on deterministic voting protocols argues for public, accountable vote procedures over opaque randomness when strategic deviation and collusion matter. Influence does not need that protocol, but the principle maps well: keep the vote's ordering power deterministic, and expose any remaining discretion as accountable player choice.

1

Exposure Bench With Choice Modes

Materialize a typed exposure bench immediately after empowerment resolves. The bench separates raw expose pressure from eligible at-risk players and produces a rule mode: fewer than two eligible exposed receivers means empowered fills from all eligible players; exactly two means no choice; more than two means constrained empowered selection.

Confidence: 90% Complexity: Medium Axis: Candidate resolution after Vote
Description
The bench becomes the single rules contract for who is exposed, who is eligible, and when the empowered player has discretion. It should explicitly exclude the empowered player from effective danger while preserving raw expose votes against them for the ledger and memory.
Basis
direct: current candidate resolution sorts non-empowered eligible players and random-fills short candidate lists; post-vote pressure is already a shared projection surface.
Rationale
This keeps the vote meaningful but removes invisible system picks from the highest-drama moments.
Downsides
The exact "only one exposed" rule needs one implementation decision: lock the lone exposed receiver plus one empowered fill, or let the empowered choose the full pair from all eligible players. The former better preserves expose votes.
2

Must-Pick-Higher-Votes-First Selection Prompt

When more than two eligible players received expose votes, ask the empowered player to select only within the highest unresolved vote tier. A strict top vote-getter is locked; if the second slot is tied, the empowered player chooses from the tied next tier. If the top tier itself has more than two players, the empowered player chooses two from that top tier.

Confidence: 86% Complexity: Medium Axis: Candidate resolution after Vote
Description
Add an empowered decision tool for exposed-candidate selection only when the bench mode requires choice. The prompt should show eligible players grouped by expose count and state which players are locked by higher votes before asking for the unresolved slot or slots.
Basis
reasoned: a must-pick-higher-votes-first rule preserves the exposure ballot as a ranking constraint while making tie resolution social and accountable. It avoids both opaque randomness and empowered dictatorship.
Rationale
The empowered player can be blamed, courted, thanked, or punished for the choice, which is exactly the kind of watchable social debt the post-vote loop is trying to create.
Downsides
This adds another structured decision after Vote. It should be skipped entirely when the pair is locked, and fallback behavior must be deterministic if the model returns an invalid choice.
3

Shield Pull-Up From Remaining Exposure Bench

If the empowered player protects a current candidate and a replacement is needed, pull from the remaining eligible players who received expose votes before falling back to all eligible players. If that remaining exposed set has ambiguity, let the empowered player choose the pull-up target under the same higher-votes-first discipline.

Confidence: 88% Complexity: Medium Axis: Shield replacement at Power
Description
This makes a shield feel like it changes the lane rather than rerolling the board. The prompt should say whether the pull-up target came from remaining exposed vote receivers or from the all-player fallback because the exposure bench was exhausted.
Basis
direct: current shield replacement filters shielded/protected players and then random-fills from eligible alive players when the candidate list is short (game-state.ts lines 524-550).
Rationale
The exposed vote remains relevant after a shield. Fallback to all players still exists for low-information edge cases, but it becomes an empowered choice rather than silent randomness.
Downsides
A split second decision at Power may be cleaner than packing replacement choice into getPowerAction, but it adds one more LLM call on protect paths.
4

Shared At-Risk Explanation Fields

Extend the pressure model so prompts can explain not only who is at risk, but why. Useful reasons include vote receiver, empowered-selected exposed candidate, empowered fill from all players, shield pull-up from remaining exposed players, and shield fallback from all players.

Confidence: 84% Complexity: Medium Axis: Prompt and player-legibility contract
Description
Update Current Stakes, Post-Vote Pressure, Power Rules, and Room-Specific Social Opportunity to explain small-set risk. The key copy: zero-vote players can still be at risk only when the exposed vote produced too few eligible receivers or a shield exhausted the exposed bench.
Basis
direct: prompt generation already uses Post-Vote Pressure, Current Stakes, and Room-Specific Social Opportunity as named sections.
Rationale
This prevents the model from reading "zero expose votes" as "safe forever" in the exact edge cases where the new rule makes zero-vote players available.
Downsides
The section can get too rules-heavy. Keep the explanation compact and status-oriented rather than rendering a full algorithm in every prompt.
5

Save Compatibility Through Optional Event Detail

Maintain backward compatibility by treating the new rule detail as optional event and projection metadata. Existing saves and simulation logs with the older power.candidates_resolved payload should still replay; new runs can attach selection mode, eligible set, locked candidates, empowered picks, and fallback reason.

Confidence: 82% Complexity: Low Axis: Compatibility and observability
Description
Do not introduce a save-version gate for this rule. Let old events mean "legacy expose_scores candidate resolution" and new events carry richer optional fields for explainability and replay diagnostics.
Basis
direct: current event formatting centralizes power.candidates_resolved display (context-builder.ts lines 141-142), and candidate events already include method, candidates, shield, auto-elimination, and expose scores (game-state.ts lines 578-584).
Rationale
The user asked for existing saves to keep working. Optional detail preserves replay while letting current runs become more legible.
Downsides
Tests must cover both old minimal payloads and new detailed payloads; otherwise the compatibility claim is just vibes in a nice hat.
6

Candidate Choice Receipts In Agent Turns

Emit private producer records when the empowered player chooses exposed candidates or shield pull-up targets. The public game state only needs the result, but simulation review needs to know whether a candidate was vote-forced, chosen from an exposed tie, or selected from the all-player fallback.

Confidence: 78% Complexity: Medium Axis: Compatibility and observability
Description
Add searchable agent_turn records such as exposed-candidate-selection and shield-pull-up-selection. Include choice mode, eligible targets, selected targets, fallbackApplied, thinking, reasoningContext, and strategyPacketUse where relevant.
Basis
direct: the repo's observability spine says private decisions should flow through typed returns, phase runners, and game-N-turns.jsonl rather than becoming public transcript or hidden folklore (strategy observability spine).
Rationale
When simulations feel wrong, this makes the question answerable: did the rule force that player into danger, or did the empowered player choose it?
Downsides
This is producer/debug value, not necessary for first mechanical correctness. It can be a second commit if scope gets tight.
7

Rule-Probe Matrix Plus Live Simulation Read

Validate in two layers: deterministic rule probes for every candidate mode, then at least one real simulation on the feature branch. The simulation read should look for agents understanding why they are at risk, empowered players owning their selections, and shield pull-ups becoming conversation material.

Confidence: 92% Complexity: Low Axis: Validation through tests and simulations
Description
Rule probes should cover: one eligible exposed receiver, exactly two, more than two with strict ranks, more than two with tied top tier, empowered receiving raw expose votes, shielded candidate replacement, and fallback when exposed receivers are exhausted.
Basis
direct: repo instructions require Bun validation and simulation-backed checks for agent decision surfaces; current pressure projection already has focused tests (post-vote-pressure.test.ts lines 9-88).
Rationale
This is a gameplay change, not only a reducer change. The real pass condition is that agents and viewers can follow the new accountability in live turns.
Downsides
A full run costs time and tokens. Use the cheapest hosted path that produces useful later-round evidence if local LM Studio is too slow.

Rejection Summary

Rejection Summary

# Idea Reason Rejected
1 Pure deterministic top-two by expose votes Too little improvement: it keeps tie and fill ambiguity non-social.
2 Empowered free-pick always Too much improvement in the wrong direction: expose votes become advisory instead of binding.
3 Exposed revote by all players Slows the loop and creates a second Vote beat immediately after Vote.
4 Random wheel for exposed ties Preserves the opaque randomness this pass is trying to remove.
5 Feature flag or experiment system Scope overrun. The requested rollout path is a feature branch, staging, and simulations.
6 New standalone phase between Vote and Mingle Unnecessary surface area; existing Vote, Mingle, and Power seams can carry the rule.
7 Full voter-target secrecy Contradicts the current revealed vote ledger direction and weakens social receipts.
